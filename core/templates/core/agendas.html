Agendas

CLIMATE ACTION

Humans are in constant pursuit of improving our lives. We often fail to notice how our actions and the choices we make affect the very planet we live on. Climate change is a real threat. It is present and looms over us as a foreboding reminder of our doom if we fail to take action. While the extinction of our species may seem too far-fetched a thought, callous choices and ignorance are paving the way to an onslaught of a dark future. This agenda calls for you to be a voice of change by discussing methods that transcend into a better future for all.

FINANCIAL INDEPENDENCE OF WOMEN

In todayâ€™s day and age, Women are under-represented at all levels of political leadership as well as managerial positions in the professional world. This poses a big social problem where women do not have the resources to be financially independent and thus feeding into the problem of gender inequality. Financially independent women create an evolved and equitable society. To shift from a chauvinistic paradigm, the barriers of discrimination need to be torn down. This agenda will dive deep into the situation and try to pose solutions for these issues at their root to strengthen the futures of women and Indian families.

RURAL ILLITERACY AND UNEMPLOYMENT

The pursuit of modernization has marginalized the weaker sections of society and has led to dwindling opportunities in terms of jobs and financial aspects. Illiteracy among the rural population contributes to the growing rate of unemployment in India, which means more people left with very few opportunities to sustain themselves and their families. This continues to ingress not only the path of development but also the economic progress of India. Young passionate individuals are in need to reinvigorate the flame of development, paving a path to progress, for the sake of society and for the sake of our fellow humans.

YOUTH PRIVACY AND SOCIAL MEDIA

Over the past few decades, the emergence of new technologies has resulted in an exponential rise in the number of active users across multiple social media platforms. Teens share a wide range of information about themselves on social media, they are exposed to inappropriate content, cyber bullying and have created a false sense of reality. While social networking sites have created multiple avenues of communication, the pitfalls related to privacy breaches and cyber attacks should raise serious questions. The agenda will discuss these problems focussing on the privacy and security issues that are inherent to social media.

ZERO HUNGER

While India produces enough food to feed its population, the country is home to 25 percent of the worldâ€™s starved population. A holistic approach to food security requires available, accessible and nutritious food in order to eradicate hunger and malnutrition in India. This agenda will seek to discuss the high rate of malnutrition, levels of stunting and food wastage, where the goal is to end hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture. Additionally, it will dive into the economics of hunger, the costs associated with it and the lack of infrastructure in rural areas to preserve food and sustainability of agriculture.
